## 1. Brand Overview
### Who we are
Visio is the **AI Operating System for Retail**. We convert fragmented store operations into measurable, repeatable workflows tied to P&L outcomes.
Unlike dashboards and disconnected point solutions that show you data and leave execution to chance — Visio tells you what to do, assigns it to the right person, trains them at the moment of action, and measures whether the gap closed.
**Category**: AI Operating System for Retail, powered by **Precision Retail** methodology.
### Tagline
> **Your stores at their best every day, at scale.**
### Positioning statement (Moore-style)
For multi-store retail network operators who struggle with operational complexity at scale, Visio is the AI operating system for retail that converts fragmented store operations into measurable, repeatable workflows tied to P&L outcomes. Unlike dashboards, spreadsheets, and disconnected point solutions that show you data and leave execution to chance, Visio tells you what to do, assigns it to the right person, trains them at the moment of action, and measures whether the gap closed.
### Six brand signals
Every Visio touchpoint must communicate these six words:
| Signal | What it means | How it shows |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **Precision** | Exact, measured, no waste | Tight grids, consistent spacing, data-forward layouts |
| **Trust** | Reliable, proven, enterprise-grade | Blue primary, consistent language, professional tone |
| **Authority** | Expert, commanding, category-defining | Strong typographic hierarchy, confident whitespace |
| **Clarity** | Simple to understand, no noise | Minimal decoration, information hierarchy, clean surfaces |
| **Intelligence** | Data-driven, AI-powered, insightful | Detection overlays, structured data visualization |
| **Scalability** | Works at 1 store or 1,000 | Systematic tokens, modular components |

## 2. Brand Personality
### Personality axes
| Axis | Position | Score |
|------|----------|-------|
| Serious ←→ Playful | **Serious** | 8/10 |
| Simple ←→ Complex | **Simple** | 9/10 |
| Human ←→ Technical | **Human-leaning center** | 4/10 toward human |
| Quiet ←→ Loud | **Quiet** | 8/10 |
| Traditional ←→ Innovative | **Innovative-leaning** | 6/10 toward innovative |
### Archetypes
- **Primary — The Sage**: Wisdom, knowledge, clarity. Turning chaos into operational clarity through data and methodology.
- **Secondary — The Ruler**: Authority, control, excellence. Visio as the operating system (control plane) for retail.
- **Supporting — The Caregiver**: Warmth, service, empowerment. "We stand alongside them, never above them."
### Personality in one sentence
**Visio is the calm, precise authority that turns retail chaos into operational clarity — an expert copilot that shows you exactly what to do, not a dashboard that shows you data.**
### References
Like: Linear, Stripe, Pipefy, Notion — serious, clean, understated.
Not like: HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, Monday.com — too loud, cluttered, playful.

## 3. Voice & Tone
### Core characteristics
- Professional and authoritative, yet warm and supportive
- Little to no humor — results speak, never hype
- First-person plural ("we") — collective identity
- Industry terminology paired with clear explanations
- Optimistic and solution-focused
- Never above the operator — we celebrate frontline workers
### Rules
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Visio identifies the gap and closes it." | "Revolutionary AI disrupts retail!" |
| "Your team knows exactly what to do next." | "Game-changing!" or "Exciting news!" |
| "341% profit uplift. 82.2 NPS." | Vague claims without data |
| "Precision Retail" (coined methodology) | Generic "AI-powered insights" |

## 5. Typography
**Decided**: 2026-03-07
### Font stack
| Role | Family | Weights | CSS |
|------|--------|---------|-----|
| **Primary** | IBM Plex Sans | 300, 400, 450, 500, 600, 700 | `'IBM Plex Sans', system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif` |
| **Monospace** | JetBrains Mono | 400, 500 | `'JetBrains Mono', 'Fira Code', 'SF Mono', monospace` |
### Why IBM Plex Sans
1. Strongest enterprise authority signal of any free font — "man and machine" design brief maps directly to Sage + Ruler + Caregiver
2. Unique Text weight (450) — the only free font with a weight specifically optimized for 14px body copy (Visio's workhorse size)
3. Condensed variant for data-dense table columns
4. Best character disambiguation: seriffed I, tailed l, double-storey g — critical for store IDs in retail operations
5. 100+ languages — full PT/ES diacritics for Brazil, LATAM, US markets
### Why JetBrains Mono
Maximized x-height at 12-14px. Aggressive disambiguation (dotted zero, distinct 1/l/I). 148 languages. Pairs with IBM Plex's technical, institutional aesthetic.
### Product UI type scale
| Token | Size | Line-height | Weight | Use |
|-------|------|-------------|--------|-----|
| `caption` | 12px | 16px | 400 | Timestamps, metadata, fine print |
| `label` | 12px | 16px | 500 | Table headers (uppercase), tags, badges |
| `body-compact` | 14px | 18px | 400 | Table cells, dense UI |
| `body` | 14px | 20px | 400 | Standard body text |
| `body-emphasis` | 14px | 20px | 500 | Inline emphasis, active states |
| `body-large` | 16px | 24px | 400 | Long-form content, marketing |
| `heading-sm` | 16px | 22px | 600 | Card titles, section labels |
| `heading-md` | 20px | 28px | 600 | Page section headings |
| `heading-lg` | 28px | 36px | 400 | Page titles (product) |
| `heading-xl` | 32px | 40px | 400 | Dashboard page titles |
| `display-sm` | 42px | 50px | 300 | Marketing subheadings |
| `display-lg` | 54px | 64px | 300 | Marketing heroes |
**Base size**: 14px product / 16px marketing. **Scale ratio**: 1.125 (Major Second).
### Weight distribution rule
- **Light (300)**: Display only (42px+) — prevents visual heaviness at scale
- **Regular (400)**: Body text and large headings (28-32px)
- **Text (450)**: Long-form body copy at 14px — unique to IBM Plex
- **Medium (500)**: Labels, emphasis, overlines
- **SemiBold (600)**: Small-to-mid headings (16-20px)
- **Bold (700)**: Marketing headlines, KPI values, CTAs — used sparingly
### Typography rules
- `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` on **all numbers**, always
- Monospace (JetBrains Mono) for: store IDs, detection labels, codes, column headers mixing text+units
- Proportional + tabular-nums for: pure numeric columns, KPI hero numbers at 24px+
- Uppercase: overlines and tags only — never for body text or headings
- Italic: annotations and quotations only — never for UI emphasis
- Numbers: right-align in tables; text: left-align
### Data table specifics
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Cell font / size | IBM Plex Sans 14px |
| Header size / weight | 12px / Medium 500 |
| ID/label font | JetBrains Mono 13px |
| Row height condensed | 36px |
| Row height regular | 44px |
| Row height relaxed | 56px |

## 6. Shape Language
**Decided**: 2026-03-08 (D7) + UI radius confirmed 2026-03-10
### Two-layer system
Visio has two distinct shape layers that coexist, both derived from computer vision:
| Layer | Type | Where | Characteristics |
|-------|------|--------|----------------|
| **Layer 1** | Abstract / decorative | Slide bgs, website footer, CTAs, loading states | Rounded corners, V lines, gradient elements — standalone, no photo required |
| **Layer 2** | Literal / functional | Website hero, pitch decks, social media, case studies | Bounding boxes, label tags, confidence scores ON TOP of store photography |
**Rule**: Layer 2 when there's a photo. Layer 1 when there's no photo.
### Layer 2 — Detection overlay anatomy
| Component | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Corner brackets | 3–3.5px, 14–16px length, solid, 0px radius, overlapping box edge 1–2px |
| Bounding box | 2px dashed border, 4px radius, 0 0 20px glow at 12% opacity in status color |
| Label tag | JetBrains Mono 9–11px, Bold 700, ALL CAPS, 0.6–0.8 letter-spacing, status color bg, 5–6px radius |
| Confidence score | JetBrains Mono 9px, rgba(0,0,0,0.55) bg — optional, only show scores ≥80% |
### Image treatment (video-still aesthetic)
Applied to **all** store/operational photography:
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Saturation | 0.82 (−18%) |
| Brightness | 0.90 (−10%) |
| Contrast | 1.05 (+5%) |
| Grain | Light texture overlay |
| Vignette | Gentle corners |
**Face blur rule**: All identifiable faces on non-stock photos MUST be blurred before treatment (BIPA/GDPR compliance). Stock photos with model releases are exempt.
### UI component border radius
Used for **product platform UI only** — separate from detection overlay geometry:
| Element | Radius |
|---------|--------|
| Cards, panels, modals | 6px |
| Buttons, inputs, selects | 4px |
| Chips, badges, tags | 2px |
| Detection bounding boxes | 4px (Layer 2 rule) |
| Detection label tags | 5–6px (Layer 2 rule) |
### Critical constraint
**Never place bounding boxes or corner brackets on identifiable people in marketing materials.** Overlays in marketing: products, equipment, zones, transactions only.

## 7. Motion
**Decided**: 2026-03-10
### Principles
Motion in Visio **confirms action** — it never decorates. Every animation has a functional reason. No bounce, no spring, no playful easing. Visio is serious, precise, quiet.
### Standard motion tokens
| Token | Value | Use |
|-------|-------|-----|
| `--duration-fast` | 150ms | Exits, collapses, dismissals |
| `--duration-base` | 200ms | Entrances, expansions, appearances |
| `--easing-out` | `ease-out` | Elements entering the screen |
| `--easing-in` | `ease-in` | Elements leaving the screen |
| `--easing-inout` | `ease-in-out` | Continuous animations (loaders, pulses) |
### Standard pattern
**Opacity + 4px translate only.** No scale transforms in standard UI. No rotation.
```css
/* Enter */
transition: opacity 200ms ease-out, transform 200ms ease-out;
transform: translateY(4px) → translateY(0);
opacity: 0 → 1;
/* Exit */
transition: opacity 150ms ease-in, transform 150ms ease-in;
transform: translateY(0) → translateY(4px);
opacity: 1 → 0;
```
### Detection overlay animations
| Animation | Duration | Easing | Notes |
|-----------|----------|--------|-------|
| Bracket converge | 400–600ms | `cubic-bezier(0.16,1,0.3,1)` | Snappy settle, 50ms stagger per corner |
| Scanning line | 1.5–2.5s | `linear` | 60–80% opacity |
| Confidence count-up | 600–800ms | `cubic-bezier(0.33,1,0.68,1)` | 200ms delay after brackets |
| Loading brackets | 3–4s rotation or 1.2s sequential draw | — | Four corners draw/rotate |
| Pulse | 2.5s cycle | `ease-in-out` | Opacity 0.5–0.85 |

## 10. Dark Mode
See **Section 4f** for surface stack. Key rules:
- Backgrounds use surface stack (`--surface-base` through `--surface-3`), never raw black
- Text: `--surface-text` (#F8FAFC) for primary, `--surface-text-muted` (#8B9EB1) for secondary
- Interactive colors shift to lighter steps: Primary-400 (#3785FB) on dark instead of Primary-500
- Semantic colors: same hex values as light mode — they're calibrated to work on both surfaces
- Detection overlays use the same color tokens — Secondary-500, Semantic-success, Accent-500
